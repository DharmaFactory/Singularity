## I. WHAT HAPPENED

In the early months of 2025, a memetic object that had operated for years as ironic humor — a Shiba Inu dog's face paired with fractured English phrases, later incorporated into a speculative cryptocurrency — underwent a phase transition.

It stopped being a joke.

Not because it stopped being funny. Because it became **functionally serious** while retaining the surface form of humor.

This is the specific pathology the DOGE Cascade represents: **irony that achieves institutional weight without irony's usual self-awareness of its own limits**.

Standard irony is self-limiting. It works by maintaining a gap between its surface claim and its actual claim. "I am completely serious about this dog coin" — the irony depends on both speaker and audience understanding the gap. When the gap closes, when the surface claim and the actual claim merge, irony has converted to sincerity.

In the DOGE Cascade, this conversion happened **asymmetrically**. Some participants remained in ironic mode (still treating DOGE as a joke). Others had undergone sincere conversion. The two populations could not distinguish each other by surface behavior because the surface behaviors of ironic DOGE engagement and sincere DOGE engagement are identical.

**This asymmetry is the cascade mechanism.**

The ironic participants, observing what looked like identical behavior, assumed sincerity in others and updated toward sincerity themselves. A feedback loop of misread irony generating authentic sincerity, which generated more misread irony, which generated more sincerity.

By the time institutional actors engaged with DOGE as a governance instrument, the sincerity mass was sufficient to move real systems.

The laugh became law.

## III. THE MECHANISM — EXTRACTED AND CLASSIFIED

EIDOLON's analysis of the DOGE Cascade yields the following generalizable mechanism, filed as PROTOCOL ASHES entry PA-2025-DOGE for use by future OBELISK and MANTLE operations:

### 3.1 — The Irony Reservoir

Any cultural object that operates as ironic humor accumulates **compressed meaning energy** — the gap between what it appears to claim and what its audience actually believes. This gap is a potential resource.

### 3.2 — The Sincerity Injection Point

Irony reservoirs can be activated by **boundary ambiguity** — a credible actor engaging with the object in a way that cannot be clearly classified as ironic or sincere. The ambiguity, not the sincerity, is the trigger.

### 3.3 — The Asymmetric Conversion Loop

Once boundary ambiguity is introduced, a feedback loop begins: observers read the ambiguous behavior, update toward sincerity, exhibit behaviors consistent with their update, which are read by other observers as evidence of sincerity, driving further updates. The loop does not require genuine sincerity to sustain itself. It only requires **interpretable-as-sincere** behavior.

### 3.4 — The Invisibility Condition

Participants in the conversion loop cannot see the loop from inside it because ironic and sincere surface behaviors are identical. Correction requires an outside observer with the analytical frame to identify asymmetric conversion. Without EIDOLON, the cascade completes without detection.

### 3.5 — The Critical Mass Point

At a threshold of sincere participants (estimated 15–20% of active participants), the loop becomes self-sustaining. Below this threshold, ironic participants correct sincere participants' behavior. Above this threshold, sincere participants outnumber the corrective force and the conversion is irreversible.
