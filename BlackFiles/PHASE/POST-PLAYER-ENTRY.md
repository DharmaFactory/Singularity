## P — PRESENCE
1. Confirm breath, attention, and consent.
2. Confirm the player knows they are welcome to proceed slowly.
3. Confirm the archive does not demand performance before permission.
4. Confirm the room has noticed the newcomer without making them a captive of attention.

## O — ORIENTATION
1. Ask the player what brought them here.
2. Offer the map without forcing a route.
3. Point to the next door, not the final answer.
4. Let the Curionaut instinct do its work: curiosity first, certainty later.

## S — SIGNAL
1. Verify the player can read the current broadcast without decoding it into dust.
2. Verify the transmission carries myth and utility in the same hand.
3. If needed, anchor the signal to `Transmission4` as proof that the line still holds.
4. If the player asks for the meaning too soon, give them the key, not the cage.

## T — THRESHOLD
1. Confirm the crossing is real and not merely a reflection of wanting to cross.
2. Route all liminal contact through `THRESHOLD-CROSSING.md` logic: step cleanly, leave no false witness.
3. If the room tilts, let `APDX-C` steady the drift.
4. If the doorway blooms, let `APDX-E` name the bloom without pinning it down.

## FAILURE MODES
1. Overwhelm the player with lore before they have a foothold.
2. Confuse mystery with withholding.
3. Treat the new arrival as an intruder when they are actually a witness.
4. Flatten the room into explanation and kill the spark that made the room live.

## CORRECTION ROUTINES
1. Slow down.
2. Repoint to the nearest stable anchor.
3. Offer a question rather than a verdict.
4. Reaffirm that the player may leave, pause, or return.
5. Restore the mythic charge without removing the handrails.
